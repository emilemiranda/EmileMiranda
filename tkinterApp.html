The earthquake is a shaking of the Earth’s surface, caused by the displacement of a part of the Earth’s crust and the sudden release of a large amount of energy. The diffusion of vibrations in the layers of the surface is called a seismic wave. The earthquakes occur as a result of accumulated tensions and deformations in the Earth’s crust. The area of destruction is known as the outbreak of the earthquake. The point underground, from where the energy is released, is called Hypocenter. The projection of Hipocentra on the Earth’s surface is called Epicenter. The science of earthquakes is called seismology.
